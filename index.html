Bill Gates

Founder: Microsoft, Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation

Early Life

  • Born October 28, 1955 in Seattle, Washington
  • Father: Lawyer, Mother: Board Member on United Way Charity
  • Competitive nature from early age
  • Interest in tinkering and computer programming
  • Attended Harvard, originally as a pre-law student
  • First company “Traf-O-Data” Was a failure”

Microsoft

  • Founded Microsoft in 1974 with Paul Allen
  • Partnered with IBM to create a user interface for their computers and MS-DOS was born
  • Released Windows in 1985, completely revolutionizing the way we, as humans, interact with computers
  • Became the world's youngest billionaire (for his time) at age 31
  • Thanks to Windows, Microsoft controlled over 90% of the market
  • Threats of antitrust litigation prompted Microsoft to invest heavily in Apple Inc., saving the company from collapse

Leadership style

  • Micromanager, but seemingly knew every facet of his business at Microsoft
  • Extremely competitive
  • Authoritative

Philanthropy

  • Bill and Melinda Gates foundation’s mission: “reduce inequities in health by developing new tools and strategies to reduce the burden of infectious diseases and the leading causes of child mortality in developing countries”
  • The Giving Pledge: leads a group of billionaires that promise to donate most, if not all of their money to charities when they die. (Warren Buffet, T.Boone Pickens, Larry Ellison, Mark Zuckerburg and many more)
  • Total value of “The Pledge”: $1015.21 Billion
